Our Story

Built from Real QA Pain,
Not a Whiteboard

BugWizard AI wasn't born in a boardroom. It was born in the trenches — after years of copy-pasting bug details, fighting inconsistent formats, and watching talented engineers waste hours on documentation instead of testing.

🧙‍♂️

From Frustration to a Full QA Operating System

It started with a simple question: "Why am I spending more time writing bug reports than actually finding bugs?"

As an SDET working with Azure DevOps and JIRA daily, the workflow was always the same — find a bug, switch tabs, fill out 15 fields, format repro steps, add screenshots, tag the right team, and pray nothing got lost. Multiply that by dozens of bugs a week.

BugWizard AI automates the entire cycle — from AI-generated bug reports to one-click submission to cloud device testing. It's the tool every QA engineer deserves but nobody had built yet.

Journey

The BugWizard Timeline

Late 2024

The Spark

After yet another week of manually formatting 40+ bug reports in Azure DevOps, the idea was born — what if AI could do this entire workflow?

Early 2025

First Prototype

A simple markdown parser that could auto-fill ADO fields. It saved 15 minutes per bug. The team started asking for copies.

Mid 2025

BugWizard AI Takes Shape

AI report generation, Ozzy AI assistant, duplicate detection, test case generator, JIRA support, and the macOS-inspired interface came together into a full QA OS.

Late 2025

Ozzy Device Farm

Cloud-based simulators and emulators joined the platform — run iOS, Android, and browser tests without buying a single device.

2026

Public Launch

BugWizard AI opens to the world. Thousands of QA engineers join the waitlist. The mission: make quality assurance effortless for every team, everywhere.
